Hypothetical PnL — taker vs. maker toy strategies

Replayed against 238 real settled markets (of 261 collected, 2026-07-07 → 07-08), using the real order books, the real Chainlink settlement outcomes, and the live-verified 0.07 crypto taker fee. Entry is ~60 s before each close; each trade stakes a fixed $100 of cost. The maker variant posts at the favorite's bid and pays zero fees — the execution mode the project's research says actually has a shot.

This is not realized PnL. Nothing traded.

No money has ever been at risk — the live module is disabled (Phase 7, ungated). This is a counterfactual replay. Several curves look profitable, but that is the trap this chart exists to show: n = 238 trades over ~2 days, and it contradicts the project's own n=200 calibration study that found no naive taker edge. The taker edge is best explained by a short trending sample plus an idealized fill (top-of-book ask, any size, no slippage — unrealistic in the final minute). The maker curve is the least-grounded of all: we collect order books, not the trade tape, so its fills are modeled, not observed. Treat the shapes, not the dollars, as the takeaway.

Did the edge survive more data?

Per-trade PnL of each strategy the first time this was run (122 trades) versus the latest run (238 trades, 261 settled markets). As the sample roughly doubled, every apparent taker/maker edge compressed toward zero — the maker variant most of all. This is the tell that the early numbers were sample/regime noise, not signal.

Zoom — the favorite-buying curves

Same data, rescaled. All three drift up, but the paths are jagged and thin. The maker curve (violet) has only 139 fills out of 238 attempts (58%), and its win rate conditional on filling is 80.6% — below the taker's 88.7% on the same favorite. That gap is adverse selection: as a maker you fill precisely when the favorite is ticking down.

Assumptions & provenance

  • Data: live-collected order books (1 Hz over each market's final 60 s) and Chainlink-settled outcomes, data/collect/btc-up-or-down-5m/.
  • Taker entry: best ask of the chosen side from the snapshot nearest T−60 s (±45 s tolerance); fill assumed at that price, full size.
  • Maker entry & fill proxy: post a buy at the favorite's best bid at T−60 s. With no trade tape collected, the fill is modeled: it counts as filled iff a later in-window snapshot shows the favorite's best bid trading below the posted price (our level got cleared). Unfilled attempts take no position. This is conservative and, being a downward-trade-through, it is exactly where adverse selection bites.
  • Fee: taker shares × 0.07 × p × (1−p), crypto_fees_v2, live-verified 2026-07-06 (config/costs.yaml). Maker fee = 0; maker rebates would only help and are deliberately not credited (conservative).
  • Not modeled: slippage, partial fills, queue position, latency, capital constraints; for the maker, whether a real resting order at that price would truly have filled. All of these push the curves down, not up.
  • Sample: 238 markets, ~2 days — far below any threshold for a deflated-Sharpe survivorship claim (Phase 4).
