# Board Memo

Format any analysis, research, or briefing into a tight one-page board memo.

## Usage

Provide the raw material — notes, research, data, a rough draft — and this skill formats it into the standard memo structure.

## Format

**MEMORANDUM**

**To**: [Board / Specified recipient]
**From**: [Author]
**Date**: [Current date]
**Re**: [Subject — one line, specific]

---

**SITUATION** (2–3 sentences)
What is happening and why it requires attention now. No background for its own sake.

**ANALYSIS** (1 paragraph, max 4 sentences)
What the data shows. What the key tension or decision point is. No hedging — take a position.

**OPTIONS**
Three options, each as a single line:
- **Option A**: [action] — [primary trade-off]
- **Option B**: [action] — [primary trade-off]
- **Option C**: [action] — [primary trade-off]

**RECOMMENDATION**
One option, stated directly. One sentence on why. No qualifiers.

**NEXT STEPS**
Three bullets. Owner, action, deadline. If owner is unknown, say "TBD."

---

**APPENDIX** (optional)
Supporting data, charts, or detail that the reader may want but shouldn't need to follow the recommendation.

---

## Rules
- Total body: under 400 words (appendix excluded)
- No passive voice in the Recommendation section
- No "in conclusion" or summary restatements
- Numbers beat adjectives: "43% increase" not "significant increase"
- Tone: direct, confident, executive-ready
