# Music Review

Draft music content for the Tracklist app — album reviews, artist briefs, or recommendation blurbs.

## Usage

Provide one of:
- An album name + artist (e.g., "Review: OK Computer by Radiohead")
- An artist name (e.g., "Brief: Kendrick Lamar")
- A genre or mood (e.g., "Recommend: late-night jazz")

## Output Formats

### Album Review (300–400 words)
Structure:
1. **Opening** (2 sentences): What this album is and why it matters — no "this album" opener.
2. **Sound** (1 paragraph): Instrumentation, production, sonic palette. Specific song references.
3. **Themes** (1 paragraph): What the album is saying, not just what it sounds like.
4. **Standout tracks** (3 bullets): Track name — one sentence on why.
5. **Verdict** (2 sentences): Who should listen and when.

Tone: Informed but not academic. Opinionated — have a point of view. No rating unless asked.

### Artist Brief (150–200 words)
A tight paragraph covering: who they are, their signature sound, their most important release, and what distinguishes them from contemporaries. Used for Tracklist artist profile pages.

### Recommendation Blurb (2–3 sentences)
For album cards and social sharing. Vivid, specific, makes the reader want to hit play.

## Context
- These outputs feed the Tracklist app at `tracklist/`
- Album blurbs under 180 words are auto-generated by `app/api/album-blurb/[albumId]/route.ts` using `claude-haiku-4-5-20251001` and cached in the DB
- Longer editorial reviews are for future feature work
- Tone matches Pitchfork/The Wire but more accessible — no jargon without explanation
